I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Mohammed VI Polytechnic University (UM6P), Ben Guerir, Morocco, where I have been working on coded distributed computing and high-performance computing since February 2026.

Prior to this, I held a Swiss Government Excellence Postdoctoral Fellowship at the University of St. Gallen, Switzerland (2024–2025), where I worked on coding theory and post-quantum cryptography under the supervision of Prof. Anna-Lena Horlemann and Prof. Joachim Rosenthal.

I completed my PhD in Mathematics in 2023 at Moulay Ismail University (ENSAM, Meknes, Morocco), under the supervision of Prof. Mustapha Najmeddine and in collaboration with Prof. Nuh Aydin. My doctoral research focused on the algebraic structure of linear codes invariant under linear and pseudo-linear transformations.

My research interests lie at the intersection of algebra, coding theory, cryptography, and their applications. More specifically, they are organized around three main research directions:

Algebraic Coding Theory: Study and construction of linear codes with cyclic and skew-cyclic structures, including polycyclic, constacyclic, quasi-cyclic, and quasi-twisted codes; investigation of rank and Hamming equivalence of skew polycyclic codes; development of LDPC and skew convolutional codes; and construction of CSS quantum codes.

Code-Based Cryptography: Design of cryptographic systems based on codes with various cyclic structures, extending beyond the classical quasi-cyclic framework (e.g., in the spirit of BIKE), with an emphasis on post-quantum security.

Coded Distributed Computing: Application of algebraic coding techniques, particularly polynomial codes, to distributed machine learning, deep learning, and distributed matrix multiplication.
